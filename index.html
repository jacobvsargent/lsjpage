Fast Facts About American Suicide

There is approximately one death by suicide every 12 minutes in the United States.

Women are approximately 3 times more likely to attempt suicide, while men are 4 times more likely to die by suicide. Men are 79% of suicides in the USA.

Whites and American Indians are at the highest risk of suicide.

Lesbian, gay, and transgender children and teens are 3 times more likely to attempt suicide.

Learn the facts at save.org.

Important Policies and Populations

Veterans and V.A. Funding

Veterans are 50% more likely to die by suicide than non-veterans.

Veterans disproportionately choose firearms as their means (79.4% versus 50.54%).

2,400 American troops have died in the Afghanistan conflict (2001-present), while 6,000 American veterans die by suicide every year.

Link: Veterans & V.A. Report via AFSP

Gun Control & Firearm Suicide Prevention

Over half of last year's American suicides involved a firearm.

States with stricter gun laws have less suicide deaths for men.

Americans die by suicide at a rate 10 times higher than other high-income nations.

The firearm suicide rate for children and teens has increased by 65% over the last 10 years.

4% of suicide attempts that do not involve a firearm end in death, while 90% of firearm suicide attempts prove fatal.

Link: Firearm Suicide Report via Everytown Research

Access to Mental Health Services

Less than half of American college students know where to go to receive mental health help.

LGBT children/teens who undergo conversion therapy die by suicide 8.4 times more often.

Close to 90% of those who die by suicide lived with a diagnosable mental health condition.

Link: Mental Health Access Report via AFSP

Joe Biden & The Democrats

Read more at Joe Biden's campaign website.

Stated Goals

  • Driving Progress to Eliminate Veterans' Homelessness and Bring Down Suicide Rates
  • Address the epidemic of suicides by firearms
  • Incentivize state 'extreme risk' (red flag) laws
  • Reinstate the Obama-Biden policy to keep guns out of the hands of certain people unable to manage their affairs for mental reasons, which President Trump reversed
  • Achieving mental health parity and expanding access to mental health care
  • Expand mental health and suicide prevention services for LGBTQ+ indvidiuals
  • Ban so-called 'conversion therapy'
